I’m a business-management consultant and software engineer: I build software that runs the business, not just software the business runs. Over a decade in enterprise engineering — from defense-grade systems to public-sector microservices — taught me where real operations break, and I now design self-hosted platforms and AI agents that close those gaps inside a client’s own stack, with a concentration in real estate and property management.

At NLMA I architected and self-host a fleet of 7+ Model Context Protocol (MCP) servers and 40+ production services that expose 20+ SaaS and data platforms as OAuth 2.1–secured tools LLM agents call directly — with autonomous multi-agent systems handling triage, reconciliation, and code fixes end to end, under human-in-the-loop guardrails.

Most AI never makes it into production. My edge is the opposite: I’m a forward-deployed engineer who ships working, self-hosted AI systems that clients fully own — code, infrastructure, and outcomes.
